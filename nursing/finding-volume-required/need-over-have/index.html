Need over have

This short video is the third of three videos in Nursing calculations: Finding the volume required. It explains the formula for calculating the quantity of a drug in solution for correct dosage by determining the stock strength and volume (have), then calculating a fraction of that stock volume for prescribed dosage (need).

If the patient is prescribed 15mg of STATE, the strength of STAT at hand is 25mg and the volume of the bottle is 10mL. What is the volume required for the patient?

Formula: VF = (need / have) x (bottleVol / 1)

Answer: 6ml = (15mg / 25mg) x (10ml / 1)

Complete the Volume Required formula by filling in the missing values.

Statement: A patient needs 2mg of Drugem, which is available in 5ml ampoules containing 250mcg.

Answer: 40ml = (2mg / 0.25mg) × (5ml / 1)

Choose the best answer.
If the strength required increases, the volume required...

  1. Increases
  2. Decreases
  3. Stays the same

Answer: 1, increases
