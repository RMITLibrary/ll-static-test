Calculating from the labels

This short video is the second of three videos in Nursing calculations: Finding the volume required. It explains how to calculate medication dosage from labels using the method of mental calculation and proportionality to get the right dosage for drugs in solution.

A vial label has a stock strength of 200mg and stock volume of 100ml. If you need 100mg, you will need to use?

Formula: VR = (SR/SS) × (SV/1)

Answer: 50 ml

A vial label has stock strength of 100 units and stock volume of 1ml. If you need 300mg, you will need to use?

Formula: VR = (SR/SS) × (SV/1)

Answer: 3 ml

A vial label has a stock strength of 120mg units and stock volume of 30ml. If you need 100mg, how many millilitres are needed?

Formula: VR = (SR/SS) × (SV/1)

Answer: 25ml

A vial label has a stock strength of 300mg units and stock volume of 2ml. If you need 75mg, how many millilitres are needed?

Formula: VR = (SR/SS) × (SV/1)

Answer: 0.5ml

A vial label has a stock strength of 250mg units and stock volume of 5ml. If you need 100mg, how many millilitres are needed?

Formula: VR = (SR/SS) × (SV/1)

Answer: 2ml
