The flow rate formula

What does "flow rate = volume/time" mean? Flow rate is determined by the volume of liquid that passes by (into a patient) within a certain time period. This is the fundamental formula for all IV problems.

This short video uses practical examples to explain the formula for calculating the flow rate of a medication infusion.

A machine can fill 1 bottle every 2 seconds. There are 20 bottles in a box.

How many bottles get filled every minute?

Answer: 30

How many bottles get filled every 10 minutes?

Answer: 300

How many boxes get filled in 1 minute?

Answer: 1.5

How many boxes get filled in 10 minutes?

Answer: 15

How long does it take to fill 500 bottles? ([ ] in seconds, and [ ] in minutes.)

Answer: 1000 in seconds, and 16.7 in minutes.

How long does it take to fill 8 boxes? ([ ] seconds, [ ] : [ ] in minutes and seconds.)

Answer: 320 seconds, 5:20 in minutes and seconds

If the machine starts at 11:00, how many boxes have been processed by 12:30?

Answer: 135

We could follow up this factory worker example with a real flow rate problem. e.g. An IV infusion is set to 30 drops per minute. The drops are macrodrops (20 drops per ml).

How many drops go by in 1 minute? How about in 10 minutes?

Answer:

  • 30 drops in 1 minute
  • 300 drops 10 minutes

How many mls go by in 1 minute? How about in 10 minutes?

Answer:

  • 1.5 mls in 1 minute
  • 15 mls in 10 minutes

How long does it take to deliver 9000 drops? minutes.

Answer: 300 minutes

How long does it take to deliver 150 ml? minutes.

Answer: 100 minutes

How many drops are delivered between 3:00 and 3:20?

Answer: 600 minutes

How many mls are delivered in that time?

Answer: 30
