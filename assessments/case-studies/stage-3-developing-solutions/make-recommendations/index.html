Make recommendations

The recommendations that you make should be based upon your solutions for each of the identified problems. Here are some examples for how you can do this.

The following example shows how headings and subheadings make the structure clear and how theory is used to support the recommendations. The shows how a student has used the solutions to make specific recommendations.

Note that recommendations are not always required as part of your assessment task. Always check your assessment criteria.

  • 1 Numbered headings and sub-headings
  • 2 Integration of theory
  • 3 Example from case

2.3. Recommendations1Screen reader users, this is a numbered heading.

2.3.1. Strategies for improving communication at HealthCo1Screen reader users, this is a sub-heading.

Strategy 1 – Provide feedback on performance1Screen reader users, this is another sub-heading.

Formalise opportunities to provide feedback on performance for all employees, including management, through regular performance reviews. Abrahms (2020) states that feedback on performance is vital to the improvement of job performance and forms part of a Human Performance System.2Screen reader users, this is integration of theory. This system would need to be implemented by the CEO and should apply across all levels of the HealthCo without exception. More holistic feedback on performance, rather than measuring productivity by 'computer hits'3Screen reader users, this is an example from the case. would assure staff that management was supportive and respectful of their skills.

Strategy 2 – Relocate management and CEO offices1Screen reader users, this is another sub-heading.

Relocate management closer to frontline staff areas and provide spaces for informal staff interactions. The CEO and senior management need to lead the relocation of management offices by moving from their current location which significantly isolates them from the clerical staff. General purpose staff rooms and office relocations should encourage interactions between staff and management.
